# Destination Research Methodology

## Research Strategy

The goal is to find destinations that mainstream travel sites miss or undervalue for this specific family. Most "best family vacation" listicles are generic — dig deeper and match to the family profile.

### Source Hierarchy

Search in this order to build a rich picture of each destination:

1. **Local tourism boards and visitor bureaus** — often have event calendars, seasonal guides, and curated itineraries that Google doesn't surface well
2. **Regional food and lifestyle blogs** — search `[destination] food blog`, `[destination] local guide`. These writers know the real spots. Adapt searches to the family's dietary needs and food interests (e.g., `[destination] best vegan food` or `[destination] craft brewery` — whatever matches their profile)
3. **Community forums and interest groups** — search based on the family's specific interests. `[destination] hiking with kids reddit`, `[destination] art scene`, `[destination] live music`, etc.
4. **Local newspapers and event calendars** — search `[destination] events [month] [year]`, `[destination] community calendar`
5. **Niche review sites** — AllTrails for hikes, Yelp for restaurants, Airbnb experience pages. Filter by whatever is relevant to this family
6. **~~maps** — verify distances, drive times, and proximity of activities to lodging

### Evaluation Criteria

Evaluate each destination against the family's stated priorities. Not every factor below will matter to every family — weight them based on what THIS family told you is important.

| Factor | What to check |
|--------|--------------|
| Travel time | Within the family's stated range; account for traffic and travel mode |
| Kid-appropriateness | Activities suited to the specific ages and interests of their kids — not generic "kid-friendly" |
| Adult appeal | Matches what the adults specifically said they enjoy |
| Lodging options | Availability within budget; features that matter to this family (kitchen, yard, pool, pet-friendly, etc.) |
| Activity density | Enough to fill the trip at the family's preferred pace |
| Weather | Check ~~weather for the specific dates; have backup options if weather-dependent |
| Local events | Bonus for events during the trip that match the family's interests |
| Dining options | Restaurants that accommodate the family's dietary needs and food interests |
| Special interests | Does the destination deliver on any specific things the family asked for? (swimming, hiking, art, history, live music, etc.) |
| Vibe match | Does the overall feel of this place match what the family described wanting? |

### Lodging: Find Actual Listings

Do not just say "Airbnbs in the $200/night range are available." Actually find them. For each destination, search and present 2-3 specific lodging options:

- Search Airbnb, VRBO, Booking.com, or hotel sites directly via web search for the family's dates, group size, and budget
- For each option, provide: name, nightly price, total cost, key features that match this family (kitchen, yard, pool, etc.), and a **direct link to the listing**
- Flag anything relevant: cancellation policy, check-in time, distance to planned activities, pet policy if applicable
- If availability can't be confirmed, say so explicitly: "Listed at $180/night but verify availability for your dates"

### Presentation Format

Present each destination as:

```
## [Destination Name]
**Travel time**: X hours from [home] by [mode]
**Vibe**: [2-3 word vibe description]
**Why it fits your family**: [2-3 sentences referencing specific things they said they want]
**Standout feature**: [The one thing that makes this special for them]
**Events during your dates**: [Any local events happening]
**Honest caveat**: [One real tradeoff to be aware of]

**Lodging options I found:**
1. [Name] — $X/night ([link]) — [1 sentence on why it fits]
2. [Name] — $X/night ([link]) — [1 sentence on why it fits]
3. [Name] — $X/night ([link]) — [1 sentence on why it fits]

<details><summary>How I researched this destination</summary>
Sources consulted: [list of blogs, sites, forums searched]
Other destinations I considered but ruled out: [brief list with reasons]
Confidence level: [High/Medium/Low — be honest about what you couldn't verify]
</details>
```

Then give a clear recommendation with reasoning tied to the family profile.

## Seasonal Considerations

- **Spring**: Mud season in mountain areas, wildflower season, fewer crowds but some attractions may not be open yet
- **Summer**: Peak season everywhere; book lodging early. Water access is premium. Look for shoulder dates (mid-week) for better pricing
- **Fall**: Foliage tourism drives up prices and crowds in many areas during peak weeks. Shoulder weeks are underrated
- **Winter**: Many outdoor attractions close or reduce hours. Indoor backup plans are essential. Ski/snow areas get crowded on weekends

## Budget-Conscious Research Tips

- Search `[destination] free things to do` for zero-cost activities matching the family's interests
- State parks are almost always cheaper and less crowded than national parks
- Lodging with kitchens saves significantly on food costs
- Many towns have free community events (concerts, markets, story times, etc.)
- Check if attractions offer online booking discounts
