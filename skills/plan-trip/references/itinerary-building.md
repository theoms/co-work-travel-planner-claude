# Itinerary Building Methodology

## Core Philosophy

The best family itinerary doesn't feel like an itinerary. It feels like a series of delightful discoveries strung together by a friend who knows the area intimately and knows what YOUR family loves. Build plans that match the family's stated pace and energy — whether that's packed with activities or spacious with breathing room.

## Day Structure: Adapt to the Family

Do not apply a rigid template. The structure of each day should emerge from the family profile:

- **If they have nappers**: Build a midday window near the lodging. But only if the family mentioned naps or has kids young enough that it's likely. Don't assume.
- **If they're early risers**: Front-load the best activities. If they're slow starters, don't schedule anything before mid-morning.
- **If they want a packed itinerary**: Fill every block with options. If they want a loose framework, give them 2-3 anchors per day and leave space.
- **If they have high-energy kids**: Build in physical activities and movement. If their kids prefer quieter experiences, don't force a "run around" slot.
- **If they have mixed ages**: Acknowledge that different kids may want different things. Suggest activities with something for everyone, or build in "split up" options where one parent takes one group.

The only universal principle: build in more flexibility than you think is needed. Families with kids almost always need it.

## Research Methodology for Activities

### Finding the Best Spots

For each activity slot, search based on the family's specific interests — not a generic list of "things to do." Tailor every search:

1. **Food & drink**: Search based on their stated food interests and dietary needs. `best [their preferred cuisine] [destination]`, `[destination] [dietary restriction] friendly restaurants`, `[destination] food blog`. Look for specific dishes or specialties the area is known for that match their tastes. **Prefer Google Maps** for restaurant research — it has the most reliable ratings, reviews, hours, and photos. Use Yelp only as a secondary source when Google Maps doesn't have enough coverage.
2. **Activities matching their interests**: Search for whatever they said they enjoy. `[destination] [their interest] with kids age [X]`, `[destination] [their hobby]`. Cross-reference niche sites for details (AllTrails for hikes, Google Maps for local businesses and attractions, etc.).
3. **Local experiences and events**: Search `[destination] events [specific date]`, `[destination] community calendar`, `[destination] local traditions`. Surface things that feel authentic and match the family's vibe.
4. **Backup plans**: Search for alternatives in case of weather, closures, or meltdowns. Match the backup to the same interests as the primary plan.
5. **"Feel like a local" spots**: Search `[destination] where locals go`, `[destination] best kept secrets`. The places tourists don't know about — but filter for relevance to this family's tastes.

### Verification Checklist

For every recommended spot, verify:

- [ ] Current operating hours (check the business's own website, not just Google)
- [ ] Day-of-week closures (many restaurants close Monday or Tuesday; farms may be weekends only)
- [ ] Reservation requirements (especially for popular spots on weekends)
- [ ] Age appropriateness for the specific kids in this family
- [ ] Dietary accommodations (if the family has restrictions)
- [ ] Accessibility for the family's needs (stroller, wheelchair, pet-friendly — whatever applies)
- [ ] Parking situation
- [ ] Cost (free, paid, or suggested donation)
- [ ] Weather dependency (flag outdoor-only spots and have backups)

Flag anything that couldn't be verified with: "**Unverified** — call ahead to confirm."

## Special Interest Research

If the family mentioned specific interests, dedicate focused research to each one. Examples:

- **Swimming**: Search for pools, lakes, swimming holes, splash pads, beaches — whatever matches the ages and comfort levels. Check depth, accessibility, lifeguards, water quality.
- **Hiking**: Match trail difficulty to the family's fitness level and youngest participant. Check stroller accessibility if relevant. Look for trails with payoffs (views, waterfalls, etc.).
- **Food/culinary**: Go deep on the local food scene matching their tastes. Find the places a food-obsessed local would recommend — not the tourist spots.
- **Art/culture**: Galleries, studios, workshops, murals, performances. Check for kid-friendly programming at museums.
- **History**: Local historical sites, tours, reenactments. Find the stories that make a place come alive.

Adapt this list entirely to what the family told you they care about.

## Presentation Format

Every place name must be a clickable link. Every price must be a real number, not a range or a vibe. Every recommendation must include evidence of why it was chosen.

```
## Day [N]: [Theme/Vibe for the Day]

### Morning
**Breakfast**: [Specific place](link to website or Google Maps) — [why it's great for this family, dietary notes, what to order]
- Price: $X-Y per person | Hours: X-Xpm | [Closed on X]
- Heads up: [Any flags]

**Activity**: [Activity name](link to website or booking page)
- What: [1-2 sentence description]
- Where: [Address](Google Maps link)
- When: [Recommended arrival time]
- Duration: [How long to plan for]
- Cost: [Actual price per person/family]
- Book here: [Direct booking link or phone number if needed]
- Why your family will love it: [Tied to their specific interests]
- Heads up: [Any flags: closures, booking requirements, parking, etc.]

<details><summary>Why I picked this</summary>
[What you searched, what alternatives you considered, why this won. Include source links — the food blog that recommended it, the AllTrails page, the Reddit thread, etc.]
</details>

### Midday
**Lunch**: [Specific place](link) — [what to order, dietary accommodations, atmosphere notes]
- Price: $X-Y per person | Hours: X-Xpm
- Menu link: [direct link to menu if available]

**Downtime/flex**: [Adapt to family — nap, chill, explore, swim, whatever fits]

### Afternoon
**Activity**: [Afternoon activity with same detail format, including links and evidence]

### Evening
**Dinner**: [Specific place](link) — [reservation notes, dietary accommodations]
- Price: $X-Y per person | Hours: X-Xpm
- Reserve: [Link or phone number]

**After dinner**: [Evening activity if the family wants one — not required]

### If plans change
[Alternative with same level of detail — links, prices, evidence]
```

### The Link Rule

If you recommend a place, you MUST include a link. Acceptable links:
- The business's own website
- Their Google Maps listing
- A booking page (OpenTable, Airbnb, Booking.com, etc.)
- A relevant review or article about the place

If you cannot find a link for a recommendation, flag it: "**No web presence found** — this is a local-only spot. Here's the address and phone number."

## Multi-Day Pacing

General guidance — but adapt to what the family told you about their pace:

- **Day 1**: Usually lighter. The family just arrived, needs to settle in, get oriented.
- **Middle days**: The biggest adventures. The family is rested and settled.
- **Last day**: Easy morning, then depart. Don't schedule anything that requires a big commitment.

If the family explicitly said they want every day packed, respect that. If they said they want lazy days, respect that too.

## Cost Tracking

For each day, tally estimated costs:

```
Day [N] estimated costs:
- Meals: $X (broken down)
- Activities: $X (itemized)
- Other: $X
- Day total: $X
```
